How to make Kinilaw

Ingredients

  • 500g fresh fish or seafood (e.g., tuna, tanigue, shrimp, or squid), cleaned and cubed
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, peeled and julienned
  • 2-3 pieces chili peppers (siling labuyo), minced (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup vinegar (preferably cane vinegar)
  • Calamansi juice (or lemon juice) from 4-5 pieces calamansi (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup coconut cream (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh cilantro or spring onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Clean and cube the fresh fish or seafood of your choice.
  2. In a bowl, combine the cubed fish or seafood, thinly sliced onion, julienned ginger, and minced chili peppers.
  3. Add the vinegar and calamansi juice to the bowl, ensuring that the fish or seafood is completely submerged in the liquid.
  4. If desired, add coconut cream to the mixture for a creamier texture.
  5. Season the kinilaw with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning according to your preference.
  6. Cover the bowl and refrigerate the kinilaw mixture for at least 1 hour to allow the flavors to meld and the fish or seafood to "cook" in the vinegar and calamansi juice.
  7. Before serving, taste the kinilaw and adjust the seasoning if necessary.
  8. Transfer the kinilaw to a serving dish and garnish with chopped fresh cilantro or spring onions, if desired.
  9. Serve the kinilaw chilled as an appetizer or main dish, typically accompanied by rice or as a pulutan (beer match) for social gatherings.
