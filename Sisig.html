How to make Sisig

Ingredients

  • 500g pork face (cheeks, ears, and jowls), boiled and chopped
  • 1 onion, minced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 pieces chili peppers (siling labuyo), minced (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon calamansi juice (or lemon juice)
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil for frying
  • Optional toppings: chopped green onions, raw egg

Instructions

  1. Boil the pork face (cheeks, ears, and jowls) until tender. Once cooked, remove from the pot, let it cool, and then chop it into small pieces.
  2. In a pan, heat some cooking oil over medium heat. Add the chopped pork face and fry until crispy and golden brown. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, sauté the minced garlic and onion until fragrant and translucent.
  4. Add the chopped chili peppers and continue to sauté for a few minutes.
  5. Return the crispy pork face to the pan and mix well with the sautéed garlic, onion, and chili peppers.
  6. Pour in the soy sauce, vinegar, and calamansi juice. Stir to combine all the ingredients.
  7. Add the mayonnaise to the pan and mix until well incorporated. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning according to your preference.
  9. Optional: For a richer flavor, crack a raw egg into the pan and mix it with the sisig while it's still hot. The residual heat will cook the egg slightly.
  10. Transfer the sisig to a sizzling plate, if available, and garnish with chopped green onions.
  11. Serve the sisig hot as an appetizer or main dish, typically accompanied by steamed rice or as a pulutan (beer match) for social gatherings.
