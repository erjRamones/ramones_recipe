Lechon Kawali

A classic Filipino dish known for its crispy yet tender pork belly. It's a popular choice for special occasions,
gatherings, and everyday meals alike, loved for its irresistible combination of crispy skin and succulent meat.
The dish is seasoned with a variety of aromatics such as garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns during the boiling process,
infusing the meat with fragrant flavors. Some recipes also call for additional seasoning blends or marinades to enhance the taste further.

Bulalo

A hearty beef soup made with beef shanks and bone marrow cooked until tender, along with corn on the
cob, cabbage, and sometimes potatoes. The broth is rich and flavorful, infused with the goodness of the beef bones.
Bulalo is often seasoned simply with salt and pepper, allowing the natural flavors of the ingredients to shine through.
It is commonly served piping hot in large bowls, accompanied by steamed rice and perhaps a dipping sauce made with calamansi
(Filipino lime) and fish sauce for added zest.

Tinolang Manok

Tinola, often referred to as Tinolang Manok, is a comforting and nourishing Filipino chicken soup that is cherished for its light
yet flavorful broth and tender meat. It is a staple dish in Filipino households, particularly during colder months or when someone is
feeling under the weather. Tinola is known for its simplicity, allowing the natural flavors of the ingredients to shine through. It is
seasoned with salt and pepper to taste, with some variations incorporating fish sauce (patis) for an extra savory kick.

Pinakbet

Pinakbet is a traditional Filipino vegetable dish that originates from the Ilocos region in the northern Philippines.
It is known for its colorful and flavorful combination of native vegetables cooked in shrimp paste (bagoong) along with garlic,
onions, and tomatoes. Pinakbet is often enjoyed as a main dish served with steamed rice. Its combination of tender vegetables,
savory shrimp paste, and aromatic seasonings creates a harmonious blend of flavors that is both comforting and satisfying.

Pinaksiw na Bangus

A Filipino dish featuring milkfish (bangus) cooked in a vinegar-based broth along with garlic, onions, ginger, and spices.
This dish is popular throughout the Philippines due to its delicious flavor and simplicity. The vinegar not only serves as a
flavoring agent but also helps preserve the fish, making Pinaksiw na Bangus a dish that can be stored for a few days without
losing its taste and quality.
