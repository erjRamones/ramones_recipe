How to make Leche Flan

Ingredients

  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (12 ounces) evaporated milk
  • 10 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar, for caramelizing

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Prepare the caramel by placing the granulated sugar in a saucepan over medium heat.
  3. Stir the sugar constantly until it melts and turns into a golden caramel syrup. Be careful not to burn the sugar.
  4. Once the sugar has caramelized, quickly pour it into individual llaneras (caramel molds) or a baking dish, tilting the molds or dish to evenly coat the bottom with caramel. Allow the caramel to cool and harden.
  5. In a mixing bowl, combine the sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, egg yolks, and vanilla extract. Mix until well combined and smooth.
  6. Strain the mixture through a fine sieve or cheesecloth to remove any lumps or air bubbles.
  7. Pour the strained mixture into the prepared llaneras or baking dish over the hardened caramel.
  8. Cover each llanera with aluminum foil or a lid, or cover the baking dish with aluminum foil.
  9. Place the llaneras or baking dish in a larger baking pan filled with hot water (about halfway up the sides of the llaneras or baking dish) to create a water bath.
  10. Bake in the preheated oven for about 45 to 50 minutes, or until the flan is set and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  11. Once done, remove the llaneras or baking dish from the oven and let the Leche Flan cool to room temperature.
  12. Once cooled, refrigerate the Leche Flan for at least a few hours or overnight to chill and set further.
  13. To serve, run a knife around the edges of the llaneras or baking dish to loosen the flan. Place a serving plate on top and quickly invert to release the flan with the caramel sauce on top.
  14. Slice and serve the Leche Flan chilled, and enjoy its creamy and caramel goodness!
