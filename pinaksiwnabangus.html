How to make Pinaksiw na Bangus

Ingredients

  • 1 whole bangus (milkfish), scaled, gutted, and cleaned
  • 1 cup vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced
  • 2-3 pieces green chili peppers (siling haba), sliced (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Cut the bangus into serving pieces and set aside.
  2. In a pot, combine vinegar, water, garlic, onion, ginger, and optional green chili peppers.
  3. Add soy sauce and brown sugar to the pot and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  5. Gently add the bangus pieces to the pot, making sure they are submerged in the vinegar broth.
  6. Cover the pot and continue to simmer over low heat for about 10-15 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through.
  7. Once the fish is cooked, taste the broth and adjust the seasoning if necessary.
  8. Remove from heat and transfer the Pinaksiw na Bangus to a serving dish.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy your tangy and flavorful Pinaksiw na Bangus!
