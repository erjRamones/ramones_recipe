Lumpiang Shanghai

Lumpiang Shanghai is a Filipino dish consisting of small, spring roll-like rolls filled with a mixture of ground pork or beef,
minced vegetables such as carrots, onions, and garlic, seasoned with soy sauce and spices. These bite-sized delights are
then deep-fried until golden brown, resulting in a crispy exterior and a flavorful, savory filling. Typically served as an appetizer or
snack, Lumpiang Shanghai is loved for its crunchy texture and delicious taste, often accompanied by a sweet and tangy dipping
sauce.

Adidas

Adidas, also known as chicken feet, is a Filipino dish characterized by tender, flavorful chicken feet cooked in a savory sauce.
The dish typically includes ingredients such as soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, onions, bay leaves, and peppercorns. Chicken feet are
simmered in this flavorful sauce until tender, creating a dish with a rich umami taste and a unique chewy texture. Adobong Adidas
is often enjoyed as a side dish or appetizer, perfect for pairing with steamed rice or as a delicious snack.

Sisig

Sisig is a sizzling Filipino dish made from chopped and seasoned pork parts, typically including pig's head and liver.
The meat is grilled or fried until crispy, then finely chopped and mixed with calamansi juice, vinegar, soy sauce, and chili peppers
for a tangy and spicy flavor. Served on a hot plate, sisig delights diners with its crispy texture and vibrant taste, often enjoyed as
an appetizer or paired with rice as a main dish.

Kinilaw

Kinilaw is a vibrant and refreshing Filipino dish that celebrates the freshness of raw fish or seafood. It involves marinating thinly
sliced pieces of fish or seafood in a mixture of vinegar or citrus juice, such as calamansi or lime, along with aromatic ingredients
like onions, ginger, and chili peppers. Sometimes, coconut milk is added to enhance the creaminess of the dish.
