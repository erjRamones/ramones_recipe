How to make Adidas

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1 kg chicken feet, cleaned and nails removed
  • 1/2 cup vinegar (preferably cane vinegar)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorns
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Water for boiling

Instructions

  1. Clean the chicken feet thoroughly by scrubbing them with salt and rinsing them with water. Trim the nails and remove any outer skin.
  2. In a large pot, place the cleaned chicken feet and add enough water to cover them. Bring the water to a boil over medium heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and let the chicken feet simmer for about 10-15 minutes to partially cook them. This step helps to soften the skin and remove any impurities.
  3. Drain the chicken feet and discard the water. Set aside.
  4. In a large pan or skillet, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped onion, and sauté until fragrant and the onions are translucent.
  5. Add the partially cooked chicken feet to the pan and sauté for a few minutes to lightly brown them.
  6. Pour in the soy sauce and vinegar. Add the bay leaves and whole peppercorns. Stir to combine all the ingredients.
  7. Cover the pan and let the chicken feet simmer over low heat for about 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken feet are tender and the sauce has thickened. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and to ensure even cooking. You can add a little water if the sauce becomes too thick.
  8. Once the chicken feet are tender, taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if necessary.
  9. Remove the bay leaves from the dish before serving.
  10. Serve the Adobong Adidas hot with steamed rice.
